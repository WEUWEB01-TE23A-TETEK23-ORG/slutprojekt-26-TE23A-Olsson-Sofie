Ten years after "The Great Hum" wiped the world’s grids and silenced the digital age, the world has grown wild, cold, and quiet. In a lonely lookout tower deep within the northern barrens, Elias, a former radio engineer, clings to a ghost. Every night, he tunes his rusted shortwave radio to the empty air, searching for a signal that shouldn't exist. Then, out of the white noise, comes a voice he hasn't heard in a decade: his daughter, Mina. But Mina isn't asking for help. She is whispering warnings. She speaks of storms before they gather and deaths before they happen. As the "Static" grows louder, Elias realizes his radio is no longer receiving broadcasts—it is catching fragments of a future that has already been decided. With a relentless group of scavengers known as The Silent Ones closing in on his signal, Elias must venture into the heart of a supernatural gale. He is chasing a voice that might be a miracle, or a trap set by the wind itself. In a world where the past is gone and the future is a warning, how far would you go to hear the truth?
